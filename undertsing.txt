Another Spe suggests mapping data sources to specific columns to improve tracking and accuracy. Disussion-2 (with stakeholders)

The meeting focuses on clarifying the **data sources, methodologies, and specific variables needed to build a baseline cash flow and Accounts Receivable (AR) forecasting model**. Data is automatically sourced from the **Oracle Analytics Cloud (OAC) ERP system**, rather than relying on manual tracking. The underlying data is categorized into two main components: **"Billed AR" (services that have been invoiced with due dates) and "Unbilled AR" (services provided but not yet invoiced)**. The stakeholders advise the data science team to ignore minor exceptions—such as Chapter 11 cases or small entities using outside local software (like Overland or Check and Maxcore)—and instead focus on the core entities processed through the ERP.

The forecasting structure was also heavily defined. The predictions must be done primarily at the **entity level, followed by the billing currency layer**, focusing especially on cross-currency exposures involving GBP, USD, and Euros. The business stakeholders confirmed they need a **single fixed-point prediction** for upcoming collections, rather than a range. For extended 90-day forecasts, the model must calculate when "Unbilled" services will turn into billed invoices based on historical "days to bill" patterns for different clients. Crucially, the **"On Account" balance—money collected from clients but not yet mapped to specific invoices—must be subtracted from the total AR** to determine the true net receivable position.
